1fe is a micro-frontend platform that standardizes development, deployment, and execution of frontend experiences. Whether you're a platform team looking to adopt 1fe for your organization or a developer building widgets, this documentation will guide you through your journey.

## <FcBookmark style={{ display: 'inline', marginRight: '0.5rem', verticalAlign: 'middle' }} /> Choose Your Path

### I want to see 1fe in action first

**→ [Live Demo: demo.1fe.com](https://demo.1fe.com)**

Experience 1fe firsthand with our interactive demo instance. This live environment showcases:

- **Dynamic Widget Loading** - See how widgets are loaded on-demand from CDN
- **Platform Utilities** - Explore built-in tools for routing, authentication, and more
- **Development Workflow** - Try the widget playground and see real-time updates
- **Micro-Frontend Architecture** - Understand how independent teams can work together
- **Production-Ready Features** - Live configurations, error boundaries, and performance optimizations

_Perfect for stakeholders who want to see 1fe's capabilities before diving into implementation details._

## <FcGraduationCap style={{ display: 'inline', marginRight: '0.5rem', verticalAlign: 'middle' }} /> Who is this for?

### Platform Teams

You're responsible for frontend infrastructure and want to standardize development across your organization. 1fe helps you provide consistent tooling, governance, and shared utilities while enabling teams to work independently.

### Frontend Developers

You're building user experiences and want to focus on creating great widgets without worrying about build systems, deployment pipelines, or platform utilities. 1fe provides the foundation so you can concentrate on your application logic.

### Technical Leaders

You're evaluating micro-frontend solutions and need to understand how 1fe compares to alternatives, what it provides out of the box, and how it fits into your organization's architecture.
